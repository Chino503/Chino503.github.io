1.01 Hypothesis, Theories and Laws

Hypothesis: a testible prediction to explain an observation.

Scientific Law: a fact or nature that's observed so often it becomes true.

  • Makes prediction about what happen.
    • Ex. the sun will rise every morning
  • But does not explain why something happens.

Scientific Measurements

Base Units: Mass – Kilograms (grams)
Length – Meter
Temperature – Kelvin (celcius)
Time – seconds

Density = mass/volume

Density Units: g/mL or g/cm2

Why is density important?
It helps identify unkown substances

Measurement Prefixes – allow scientists to easily convert and represent diferent magnitudes of a number using base 10.
Kilo, Hecto, Deka, Base, Deci, Centi, Mili

Conversion Factors:

100 centi = 1 base
1000 milo = 1 base
1000 base = 1 kilo

What does every meausement have?

  • a number (magnitude)
  • a unit (label)

When measuring:
Accuracy – how closely a measurement is to the correct (true) value.
Precision – when things repeat over and over again.
Percent Error – a way of expressing accuracy. 

  • 	|theoretical - experinmental| x 100%
	
    
	theoretical
  • Theoretical value – correct value.
  • Experimental value – value when you do the lab experiment.

Scientific Notation – used to express very large or very small numbers
Format: (# between 1 and 9.9) x 10 exponent ex: 2.6 x 10 3 is 2.600

a positive exponent represents a number greater than one.
a negative exponent represents a number less than one.

Graphing
Most commont type of graph in chemistry – line graph
When contructing a graph:

  • Title
  • Label x and y axis
  • Scale axis evenly and show units
Interpreting relationship from graphs:
Direct Proportions– both variables increase or decrease.
Indirect Proportions– one variable increases as the other decreases.
